West-Siang

The Akashganga Temple, located in Arunachal Pradesh, is a significant pilgrimage site with natural and spiritual importance.

Dispur

Kaziranga National Park, located in the Indian state of Assam, is one of the most renowned and significant wildlife sanctuaries in the world. Kaziranga National Park stands as a symbol of successful wildlife conservation in India, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and rich biodiversity. Its commitment to preserving the one-horned rhinoceros and other endangered species makes it a vital sanctuary for wildlife enthusiasts and conservationists alike.

Bodhgaya

Bodhgaya is one of the most significant and revered pilgrimage sites for Buddhists around the world. It is located in the Gaya district of the Indian state of Bihar. Bodhgaya is a profound symbol of the Buddha's enlightenment and continues to be a vital center for spiritual practice and pilgrimage.

Raipur

Ambuja City Center is a significant urban development project that aims to create a vibrant mixed-use space within a city. While there might be various projects under the name "Ambuja City Center" depending on the location, typically, such developments integrate commercial, residential, and recreational spaces in a central location. These centers often include shopping malls, office spaces, residential apartments, entertainment zones, and sometimes even educational institutions or healthcare facilities, all within close proximity to each other.

Baga

Baga Beach stands out for its lively atmosphere, diverse activities, and beautiful coastal scenery, making it a top destination in Goa for both relaxation and entertainment.

Gandhinagar

The Statue of Unity is a colossal statue located in the Indian state of Gujarat, near the Sardar Sarovar Dam on the Narmada River. It is dedicated to Vallabhbhai Patel, who played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence and subsequently in the integration of the princely states into the Indian Union after independence.

Chandigarh

Gurudwara Sri Nada Sahib is a prominent Sikh shrine located in Panchkula district of the Indian state of Haryana, near Chandigarh. It holds historical significance in Sikhism as it is associated with Guru Gobind Singh Ji, the tenth Sikh Guru.

Shimla

Shimla is a picturesque hill station located in the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Overall, Shimla's natural beauty, colonial heritage, and pleasant climate make it a popular destination for tourists seeking relaxation, adventure, and a glimpse into India's colonial past.

Deoghar

Deoghar, also known as Baidyanath Dham, is a significant pilgrimage center located in the Santhal Parganas division of the Indian state of Jharkhand. Overall, Deoghar holds immense religious significance for Hindus and is an important pilgrimage destination attracting devotees and tourists throughout the year, especially during religious festivals and events.

lalbagh

Lalbagh Botanical Garden, often simply referred to as Lalbagh, is a famous botanical garden located in Bengaluru (Bangalore), Karnataka, India. Overall, Lalbagh Botanical Garden is a cherished green space in Bengaluru, offering a peaceful retreat amidst the bustling city and showcasing the rich botanical heritage of the region.

Thiruvananthapuram

The Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a prominent Hindu temple located in Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city of the Indian state of Kerala. The Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of Kerala's rich cultural and religious heritage, attracting pilgrims and tourists from around the world.

Gwalior

Gwalior Fort is a historic hill fort located in the city of Gwalior in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. Gwalior Fort stands as a testament to the rich cultural heritage and architectural prowess of India, attracting tourists and history enthusiasts from around the world.

Mumbai

The Gateway of India is an iconic monument located in Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. the Gateway of India stands as an enduring symbol of Mumbai and India's colonial past, while also serving as a vibrant hub of activity and a testament to the city's cosmopolitan character.

Imphal

Kangla Sha or Kangla Palace is a significant historical site located in Imphal, the capital city of Manipur, India. Kangla Sha is not just a historical site but a symbol of Manipur's rich heritage and cultural legacy. It continues to be a source of pride and reverence for the people of Manipur and serves as a reminder of the region's glorious past.

Umlarem

It is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and anyone seeking solace amidst nature's splendor.

Aizwal

Solomon's Temple is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of faith, community, and cultural pride for the people of Mizoram. Its grandeur and significance make it a must-visit destination for tourists exploring the region.

Puri

Amritsar

Located in Amritsar, Punjab, India, it is a major pilgrimage site and a symbol of Sikhism.Harmandir Sahib is not only a spiritual center for Sikhs but also a symbol of universal brotherhood and peace, drawing millions of visitors from around the world every year.

Pink City-Jaipur

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is a renowned palace in Jaipur, the capital city of the Indian state of Rajasthan. Hawa Mahal stands as a testament to the architectural genius and cultural richness of the Rajput era, making it a must-visit landmark in Jaipur.

Ravangla

Buddha Park, also known as Buddha Park of Ravangla, is a prominent tourist attraction located in Ravangla, a picturesque town in the Indian state of Sikkim. Buddha Park Sikkim is not only a tourist attraction but also a sacred and tranquil sanctuary where visitors can connect with nature, find inner peace, and experience the spiritual essence of Buddhism amidst the majestic beauty of the Himalayas.

Madhurai

The Meenakshi Amman Temple is a historic Hindu temple located in the city of Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India. The Meenakshi Amman Temple is a cherished treasure of Madurai and a testament to the devotion, artistry, and spiritual fervor of its creators and worshippers throughout the centuries.

Hyderabad

Charminar is an iconic monument located in the heart of the city of Hyderabad, India.
The name "Charminar" translates to "Four Towers" in Urdu, referring to its distinctive architectural style featuring four grand arches facing the four cardinal directions. It was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the founder of Hyderabad, to commemorate the end of a deadly plague that ravaged the city. The monument stands at the intersection of four major roads and has become a symbol of Hyderabad's rich history and culture. Each of its four stories has a gallery from which visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the city. The Charminar is also surrounded by bustling markets known for their vibrant atmosphere and diverse range of goods, making it a popular tourist destination. Over the centuries, Charminar has witnessed the growth and transformation of Hyderabad into a major metropolitan city while retaining its status as a beloved landmark and cultural icon. It continues to be a symbol of the city's heritage and architectural brilliance, attracting visitors from all over the world.

Tripura

Agartala is the capital city of the northeastern state of Tripura, India. Situated near the Bangladesh border, Agartala serves as the political, cultural, and economic center of Tripura. Agartala serves as a vibrant hub in the northeastern region of India, offering a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

Agra

The Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic and recognizable monuments in the world, located in Agra, India. The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. It took over 20,000 artisans and laborers around 22 years to complete the construction, which finished in 1653.

Uttarakhand

The Valley of Flowers National Park is not located in the United Kingdom; rather, it is situated in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. The Valley of Flowers National Park is not only a haven for nature lovers and botanists but also a pilgrimage site for trekkers seeking to immerse themselves in the beauty of the Himalayas and its floral treasures.

Kolkata

The Howrah Bridge is an iconic cantilever bridge located in Kolkata, India, spanning the Hooghly River. The bridge was commissioned in 1943 during British rule in India and was completed in 1942. It replaced a floating pontoon bridge that was inadequate to handle the increasing traffic between the two cities.
